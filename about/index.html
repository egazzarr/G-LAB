What is GAZZARRINI LAB?

Gazzarrini Lab is a science communication studio that strives to visually explain scientific concepts, especially topics in physics, through 3D animations, videos, stories and images. Quantum bits become animated 3D objects, computers from the 1980s get animated to tell their history, scientists get interviewed to uncover their secrets. Elena, an Italian physicist with a Msci in Physics from King's College London, founded Gazzarrini Lab in 2024 after having worked as a DevOps engineer first, and as a science communicator later, in the IT department at CERN, the largest particle accelerator in the world. Her work focuses on collecting information, distilling it, and choosing the best strategy to communicate it simply and beautifully. She has been involved in content curation for interactive science exhbitions, has created 2D and 3D animations to represent physics concepts and is continuously writing science articles and developing novel concepts for science documentaries.
